Our Intensive Care Facilities

Intensive Care Unit (ICU)

Adult Critical Care · 24×7 Intensivist-led

The Medical & Surgical ICU at VRIMS is equipped with cutting-edge life-support technology and managed by trained intensivists and critical care nurses around the clock. Every bed is centrally monitored with multi-parameter monitors to ensure optimal patient outcomes.

  • Ventilator support with advanced modes (CPAP, BiPAP, volume/pressure control)
  • Central line insertion, arterial line monitoring
  • Continuous multi-parameter vital monitoring
  • Haemodynamic monitoring and management
  • Point-of-care testing (ABG, electrolytes, lactate)
  • Bedside echocardiography & bronchoscopy
  • Post-operative care for major surgeries
  • Sepsis protocol and evidence-based bundles

Neonatal Intensive Care Unit (NICU)

Newborn Critical Care · 0–28 days

VRIMS NICU provides specialised intensive care for sick newborns including premature infants, babies with low birth weight, and neonates with birth complications. Our neonatology team provides compassionate, evidence-based care using modern incubators and life-support systems.

  • Servo-controlled incubators for thermoregulation
  • High-frequency oscillatory ventilation (HFOV)
  • Surfactant therapy for premature lungs
  • Total parenteral nutrition (TPN) support
  • Phototherapy units for neonatal jaundice
  • Umbilical catheterisation & vascular access
  • Kangaroo Mother Care (KMC) facility
  • Neonatal transport incubator

Paediatric Intensive Care Unit (PICU)

Children's Critical Care · 1 month – 18 years

VRIMS PICU is a dedicated critical care facility for critically ill children from infancy through adolescence. Our paediatric intensivists and specialised nursing team deliver age-appropriate, family-centred intensive care using equipment specifically sized for children.

  • Paediatric ventilators with age-specific modes
  • Non-invasive positive pressure ventilation (NIPPV)
  • Continuous renal replacement therapy (CRRT)
  • Intraosseous access & vascular cut-down
  • Paediatric crash cart & resuscitation trolley
  • Enteral and parenteral nutrition
  • Family-centred care with parent counselling
  • Child life support (PALS) certified staff

ICU Admission

ICU Admission Process

1

Emergency / OPD Referral

Patient referred from Emergency, OPD, or ward based on clinical assessment by treating physician.

2

Intensivist Assessment

Dedicated ICU intensivist evaluates and confirms need for intensive monitoring or ventilatory support.

3

Consent & Documentation

Informed consent obtained from patient/guardian. Medical history and admission papers completed.

4

Bed Allotment

Appropriate ICU/NICU/PICU bed assigned based on condition, age group, and availability.

5

Family Communication

Daily doctor-family briefing at fixed timing. One attendant permitted during visiting hours.
