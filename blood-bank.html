Our Services

Blood Bank Services

Whole Blood Collection

Safe voluntary blood donation with comprehensive pre-donation screening and donor counselling.

Component Separation

Advanced component separation: Packed RBCs, Platelets (Random & Single Donor), FFP, and Cryoprecipitate.

Blood Grouping & Typing

ABO & Rh typing, extended red cell antigen phenotyping for complex transfusion cases.

Cross Matching

Immediate spin, electronic, and full serological cross-matching for all blood requests.

Infectious Disease Screening

Mandatory TTI screening: HIV, HBsAg, HCV, Malaria, Syphilis (VDRL) for every unit.

Platelet Concentrate

Random Donor Platelets (RDP) and Single Donor Platelets (SDP) via apheresis available.

Fresh Frozen Plasma

FFP for coagulation factor replacement in bleeding disorders and liver disease patients.

Neonatal Transfusion

Irradiated and CMV-negative blood for neonatal and paediatric transfusion requirements.

Pre-transfusion Testing

Comprehensive serological testing including antibody screening and direct antiglobulin test (DAT).

Apheresis Services

Therapeutic plasma exchange and plateletpheresis for eligible patients.

Blood Storage

Walk-in cold rooms and validated blood bank refrigerators maintaining 2–6°C continuously.

Emergency Dispatch

Blood units dispatched within 30 minutes for emergency surgeries and trauma cases.

Donate Blood, Save Lives

Blood Donation Programme

Eligibility Criteria for Donation

One blood donation can save up to 3 lives. Join our voluntary blood donor registry and help patients in need. Walk-in donors are always welcome during donation hours.

  • Age between 18 and 65 years
  • Weight 45 kg or more
  • Haemoglobin ≥ 12.5 g/dL
  • Blood pressure within normal limits
  • No fever or active infection at the time of donation
  • Minimum 3 months gap since last donation
  • No history of HIV, Hepatitis B/C, Syphilis, or Malaria in recent months
  • Not pregnant or lactating (for female donors)
  • Valid photo ID proof required

Donation Process

1

Registration

Present at Blood Bank counter with photo ID. Fill donor registration form.

2

Pre-Donation Screening

Medical officer checks BP, pulse, haemoglobin and asks health-related questions.

3

Blood Collection

350–450 mL of blood collected in 7–10 minutes using sterile, single-use bags.

4

Rest & Refreshment

Donor rests for 10–15 minutes. Light refreshments (juice, biscuits) provided.

5

Donor Certificate

Donation certificate issued. Donor card with blood group details provided.

Blood Request

How to Request Blood

1

Doctor's Requisition

Treating doctor fills requisition form with patient details, blood group, and units required.

2

Sample Collection

5 mL patient blood sample collected in EDTA & plain tube with proper labelling.

3

Compatibility Testing

Blood grouping and cross-matching performed. Takes 30–60 minutes in routine cases.

4

Issue of Blood

Compatible blood unit issued with transfusion record. Patient attendant must carry ID.

5

Transfusion Monitoring

Nurse monitors patient vitals throughout transfusion for any adverse reactions.
