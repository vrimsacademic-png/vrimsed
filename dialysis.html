About the Unit

Advanced Renal Care at VRIMS

The Dialysis Unit at VRIMS is equipped with modern haemodialysis machines and managed by a dedicated team of nephrologists and dialysis technicians. We provide safe, comfortable, and effective renal replacement therapy to both chronic and acute kidney failure patients.

Our unit is empanelled under the Bihar government's free dialysis scheme (Mukhyamantri Nishchay Swasthya Sewa) making dialysis accessible and affordable to all patients from East Champaran and surrounding districts.

What We Offer

Dialysis & Renal Services

Haemodialysis (HD)

Standard intermittent haemodialysis using modern Fresenius/B.Braun machines with online Hdf option.

Acute Dialysis

Emergency dialysis for acute kidney injury (AKI) in ICU patients, poisoning, and fluid overload.

Peritoneal Dialysis

CAPD training and support for eligible patients who prefer home-based peritoneal dialysis.

CRRT

Continuous Renal Replacement Therapy for critically ill ICU patients with haemodynamic instability.

AVF / AVG Creation

Arteriovenous fistula and graft creation for long-term haemodialysis access by vascular surgery team.

Dialysis Adequacy Testing

Regular Kt/V monitoring and URR measurement to ensure optimal dialysis adequacy for each patient.

Nephrology Consultation

Dedicated nephrology OPD for CKD management, anaemia of CKD, bone mineral disease, and more.

Dietitian Counselling

Personalised renal diet advice by trained dietitian — low potassium, phosphate, and fluid management.

Starting Dialysis

How to Start Dialysis at VRIMS

1

Nephrology OPD

Consult nephrologist with all previous medical records, lab reports, and imaging.

2

Pre-Dialysis Workup

Blood tests: CBC, BMP, viral markers (HIV, HBsAg, HCV), coagulation profile, ECG, chest X-ray.

3

Vascular Access

Nephrologist advises appropriate access — temporary catheter, AV fistula, or PD catheter.

4

Registration & Consent

Dialysis registration done, informed consent taken, dry weight and treatment prescription noted.

5

Schedule Allotment

Regular session slot allotted based on frequency (usually 2–3×/week) and machine availability.

6

First Session

First dialysis session performed under close monitoring. Vitals checked every 30 minutes.

Patient Education

Understanding Kidney Disease

What is CKD?

Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) is a long-term condition where kidneys gradually lose their ability to filter waste and excess fluid. It is classified into stages 1–5 based on GFR levels.

Warning Signs

Swelling in legs/face, decreased urine output, fatigue, nausea, itching, high blood pressure, and shortness of breath may indicate kidney problems.

Diet in CKD

Limit potassium (banana, potato), phosphorus (dairy, nuts), sodium (salt), and fluids depending on your stage of CKD. Follow dietitian's personalised plan.

Medications

Never stop prescribed medications. Avoid NSAIDs and nephrotoxic drugs. Report all OTC medicines to your nephrologist.

Home Care

Monitor blood pressure daily, maintain fluid intake diary, care for AV fistula, and attend all scheduled dialysis sessions without skipping.

Regular Monitoring

Monthly blood tests (renal function, CBC, electrolytes), quarterly bone profile, and regular review by nephrologist to assess dialysis adequacy.
